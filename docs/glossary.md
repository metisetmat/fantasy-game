# Glossary

This glossary is the official terminology contract for tactical reports, interaction specs, and calibration prompts.

## Weak Side

The side or corridor the defending team has under-protected. A weak side can overlap with the open side, but only when defensive coverage is actually weak.

## Short Side

The side with less attacking space relative to the ball. It is an attacking spatial reading, not automatically a weak side.

## Open Side

The side with more available attacking space or weaker immediate pressure after the defensive block shifts.

## Overload

A local numerical or structural advantage, such as 2 attackers versus 1 defender in or near a target zone.

## Line Bypassed

A defensive line is bypassed when ball movement or carrying puts defenders behind the ball or removes their immediate goal-side relevance.

## Rest Defense

The security structure an attacking team keeps behind or level with the ball to prevent dangerous counterattacks.

## Front-Foot Ball

An attacking state where the ball carrier or support wave has advanced through pressure and the next action can happen against a retreating defense.

## Support Triangle

A stable support shape around the ball carrier with at least two connected outlets at different angles or depths.

## Pressing Trap

A coordinated defensive structure that guides the ball into a constrained zone, blocks exits, and increases turnover pressure.

## Cover Shadow

The space or passing lane blocked by a defender's positioning while they pressure or screen another option.

## Danger Phase

A persistent tactical state where the attacking team has reached immediate scoring pressure and the phase must resolve through finishing, emergency defense, turnover, second chance, or ball exit.

## Chaotic Attacking Advantage

A state where chaos favors the attacking team because the defensive line is bypassed, support remains connected, and defenders are delayed or recovering.

## Delayed Defender

A defender who is behind the current tactical action and cannot immediately restore compactness or goal-side pressure.

## Eliminated Defender

A defender who has been removed from immediate relevance by a pass, carry, long play, or structural bypass.
