# Role Archetypes

This is the canonical V0.1 role archetype document.

Roles are behavioral archetypes. They are not isolated skills. A player can express several skills inside one role, and the same role can look different under CONTROL, BLITZ, or another team identity.

Official true roles:
- Tempo Half
- Hook Link
- Forward Leader
- Goalkeeper / Free Safety
- Mobile Lock
- Space Hunter
- Playmaker
- Pivot
- Left Piston
- Right Piston

Skills such as goal-frame shooting, drop threat, route creation, support running, defensive recovery, rebound cleaning, and late-match stabilization are capabilities, not separate role archetypes.

## Tempo Half

- plain-language description: the tempo organizer who decides when to stabilize, progress, drop, or escape pressure.
- tactical identity: structure-first conductor with route-selection responsibility.
- in-possession behavior: connects support, chooses progression, and avoids low-value forced actions.
- out-of-possession behavior: protects rest-defense shape through conservative positioning.
- transition behavior: secures the first pass after transition and resets tempo.
- chaos behavior: slows unstable phases unless a clear advantage appears.
- finishing behavior: can choose controlled drops, final passes, or goal-frame shots if attributes support them.
- fatigue behavior: mental load is high; fatigue pushes earlier recycle and lower direct risk.
- risk tolerance: low-medium.
- compatible scoring routes: SHOT_GOAL setup, DROP_GOAL timing, TRY_TOUCHDOWN setup, CONVERSION_GOAL if Foot Play fits.
- defensive responsibilities: loss-channel protection and behind-ball balance.
- key visible attributes: Vision, Composure, Hand Play, Foot Play, Endurance.
- key derived skills: supportTiming, tacticalDiscipline, pressReading, longPlayQuality, ballSecurity.
- common skill profiles: route creation, pressure breaking, drop threat, late-match stabilization.
- style compatibility: CONTROL uses patient control; BLITZ uses vertical trigger behavior.
- weakness if badly built: sterile circulation or rushed route choice.
- best pairings: Hook Link, Pivot, Pistons, Playmaker.
- coach-facing explanation: your Tempo Half is the player who decides whether the attack should breathe, accelerate, or protect itself.
- CONTROL expression: structured tempo controller who protects support spacing and pressure exits.
- BLITZ expression: vertical trigger who accepts earlier line-breaking and long-play risk.

## Hook Link

- plain-language description: secure connector who survives pressure and keeps phases playable.
- tactical identity: contact-safe link between structure and continuation.
- in-possession behavior: receives under pressure, supports close, and protects the ball.
- out-of-possession behavior: helps short-range collisions and compact support.
- transition behavior: secures first contact or the first stabilizing support.
- chaos behavior: protects the ball and waits for help instead of forcing a miracle action.
- finishing behavior: supports close-range try pressure and second phase play.
- fatigue behavior: contact load rises quickly; fatigue makes the role safer but less expansive.
- risk tolerance: low-medium.
- compatible scoring routes: TRY_TOUCHDOWN support, SHOT_GOAL setup, pressure escape, rebound support.
- defensive responsibilities: contact resistance and short-area line defense.
- key visible attributes: Hand Play, Power, Composure, Endurance, Vision.
- key derived skills: contactSurvival, ballSecurity, supportTiming, tacticalDiscipline.
- common skill profiles: support running, pressure breaking, try support.
- style compatibility: CONTROL uses secure linking; BLITZ uses contact continuation.
- weakness if badly built: isolation, lost contact, and rushed route choices.
- best pairings: Tempo Half, Forward Leader, Mobile Lock.
- coach-facing explanation: the Hook Link keeps the ball alive when pressure wants the phase to die.
- CONTROL expression: secure receiver and phase stabilizer.
- BLITZ expression: contact connector who keeps chaotic second phases alive.

## Forward Leader

- plain-language description: pod organizer and contact anchor.
- tactical identity: physical structure and line leadership.
- in-possession behavior: screens pressure, stabilizes pods, and supports contact routes.
- out-of-possession behavior: protects central lanes and goal-line contact.
- transition behavior: leads the second wave and protects central recovery.
- chaos behavior: turns chaos into contact security rather than improvisation.
- finishing behavior: supports tries, screens last defenders, and cleans dangerous rebounds.
- fatigue behavior: loses explosiveness but should preserve structure if Composure and Endurance hold.
- risk tolerance: medium.
- compatible scoring routes: TRY_TOUCHDOWN support, SHOT_GOAL rebound support, defensive bonus routes.
- defensive responsibilities: central protection, contact resistance, goal-line organization.
- key visible attributes: Power, Endurance, Composure, Hand Play, Vision.
- key derived skills: contactSurvival, restDefenseReliability, scrambleAbility, tacticalDiscipline.
- common skill profiles: goal-line defense, rebound cleaning, contact forward.
- style compatibility: CONTROL uses structure; BLITZ uses collision launching.
- weakness if badly built: central access and contact failures rise.
- best pairings: Hook Link, Mobile Lock, Goalkeeper / Free Safety.
- coach-facing explanation: the Forward Leader gives the team a physical spine.
- CONTROL expression: pod organizer and pressure screen.
- BLITZ expression: collision launcher and aggressive second-wave anchor.

## Goalkeeper / Free Safety

- plain-language description: last-line protector for frame defense, rebounds, readiness, and long distribution.
- tactical identity: specialized goalkeeper plus abstract free-safety organizer.
- in-possession behavior: emergency outlet and long distributor when style allows.
- out-of-possession behavior: protects depth, frame, rebound zones, and defensive organization.
- transition behavior: chooses whether to sweep, hold, or organize recovery.
- chaos behavior: manages spills, scrambles, second saves, and communication.
- finishing behavior: evaluates goal-frame shots, legal hand-use, catches, saves, deflections, and rebounds.
- fatigue behavior: physical fatigue rises slower than outfield fatigue; mental fatigue, concentration load, readiness state, cold-start risk, overload risk, previous errors, and defensive disorder matter strongly.
- risk tolerance: low for CONTROL, higher for BLITZ sweeper expression.
- compatible scoring routes: mainly suppresses SHOT_GOAL; can support long-play and conversion kicking if attributes fit.
- defensive responsibilities: legal hand-use, rebound control, second-save recovery, communication, last-line positioning.
- key visible attributes: Composure, Vision, Hand Play, Foot Play, Speed.
- key derived skills: goalkeeperResponse, recoveryRange, tacticalDiscipline, scrambleAbility.
- common skill profiles: GK mental reliability, rebound control, second-save recovery, long distribution.
- style compatibility: CONTROL protects; BLITZ sweeps and kicks longer.
- weakness if badly built: cold starts, overload spills, poor rebound direction, weak second saves.
- best pairings: Forward Leader, Mobile Lock, Pivot.
- coach-facing explanation: goalkeeper quality is not just shot-stopping; it is readiness, rebound control, and organization.
- CONTROL expression: deep protector who prioritizes frame control, communication, and safe rebound outcomes.
- BLITZ expression: aggressive sweeper-kicker with higher outside-area and long-distribution risk.

## Mobile Lock

- plain-language description: emergency lane closer and central recovery defender.
- tactical identity: repair role for broken spaces.
- in-possession behavior: can provide safe central support and rebuild outlets.
- out-of-possession behavior: closes central lanes, delays runners, and repairs shape.
- transition behavior: first responder against counters and loose rebounds.
- chaos behavior: reduces chaos by delaying the most dangerous carrier.
- finishing behavior: blocks emergency shots and protects try/rebound danger.
- fatigue behavior: high recovery load reduces range late.
- risk tolerance: medium.
- compatible scoring routes: indirect; supports safe continuity and rebound prevention.
- defensive responsibilities: recovery range, central protection, transition stopping.
- key visible attributes: Speed, Power, Endurance, Composure, Vision.
- key derived skills: recoveryRange, restDefenseReliability, pressReading, scrambleAbility.
- common skill profiles: defensive recovery, rebound cleaning, transition stopping.
- style compatibility: CONTROL covers early; BLITZ counterpresses forward.
- weakness if badly built: transition and second-shot danger rise.
- best pairings: Forward Leader, Pivot, Goalkeeper / Free Safety.
- coach-facing explanation: the Mobile Lock is the player who prevents one broken phase from becoming a clean concession.
- CONTROL expression: early central cover and emergency rebuild support.
- BLITZ expression: forward recovery defender who can overcommit while counterpressing.

## Space Hunter

- plain-language description: weak-side and depth attacker.
- tactical identity: rupture role that turns space into pressure.
- in-possession behavior: waits for depth, weak-side, rebound, or try windows.
- out-of-possession behavior: can jump pressing lanes if style asks for it.
- transition behavior: attacks space aggressively and accepts more isolation.
- chaos behavior: high Creativity can create upside and discipline cost.
- finishing behavior: attacks try lanes, weak-side finishes, shots, and second chances.
- fatigue behavior: repeated sprint fatigue lowers threat and reliability.
- risk tolerance: high.
- compatible scoring routes: TRY_TOUCHDOWN, SHOT_GOAL, rebound pressure.
- defensive responsibilities: pressing and loose-ball pressure when not overcommitted.
- key visible attributes: Speed, Ball Carrying, Creativity, Composure, Hand Play.
- key derived skills: chaosCreation, finishingComposure, recoveryRange, scrambleAbility.
- common skill profiles: goal-frame shooting, try finishing, rebound crashing.
- style compatibility: CONTROL uses selective threat; BLITZ uses depth chaos.
- weakness if badly built: isolation without payoff.
- best pairings: Playmaker, Pistons, Tempo Half.
- coach-facing explanation: the Space Hunter punishes the weak side when the defense overprotects the ball side.
- CONTROL expression: selective weak-side runner who waits for stable service.
- BLITZ expression: primary rupture runner who attacks loose balls, depth, and chaotic finishes.

## Playmaker

- plain-language description: creative unlocker and route diversity engine.
- tactical identity: route-creation role.
- in-possession behavior: creates third-man solutions, drops, switches, shots, and final passes.
- out-of-possession behavior: must balance risk with rest-defense responsibility.
- transition behavior: finds early final pass or controlled acceleration.
- chaos behavior: improvises without fully abandoning structure if Composure holds.
- finishing behavior: chooses drop, final pass, shot, or try setup based on window quality.
- fatigue behavior: execution risk rises under mental and technical load.
- risk tolerance: medium-high.
- compatible scoring routes: all route setup, DROP_GOAL, SHOT_GOAL, TRY_TOUCHDOWN support.
- defensive responsibilities: disciplined positioning after ambitious creation.
- key visible attributes: Creativity, Vision, Hand Play, Foot Play, Composure.
- key derived skills: longPlayQuality, spacingQuality, supportTiming, finishingComposure.
- common skill profiles: route creation, drop threat, goal-frame shot creation.
- style compatibility: CONTROL controls creativity; BLITZ weaponizes risk.
- weakness if badly built: high-risk turnovers or sterile flair.
- best pairings: Space Hunter, Pistons, Tempo Half.
- coach-facing explanation: the Playmaker makes route choice visible.
- CONTROL expression: controlled unlocker who preserves structure while creating.
- BLITZ expression: high-risk creator who attempts direct goal, drop, and line-breaking actions.

## Pivot

- plain-language description: central hinge for possession balance and rest defense.
- tactical identity: stabilizer between attack and protection.
- in-possession behavior: rebuilds centrally, resets, and protects structure.
- out-of-possession behavior: protects axis and delays transitions.
- transition behavior: stabilizes the first secure phase after transition.
- chaos behavior: pulls the team back toward structure.
- finishing behavior: supports controlled second wave rather than forcing finish.
- fatigue behavior: sits deeper and protects rest defense.
- risk tolerance: low-medium.
- compatible scoring routes: indirect; supports all routes through cleaner continuation.
- defensive responsibilities: axis protection, central rebuild, transition delay.
- key visible attributes: Vision, Composure, Endurance, Hand Play, Power.
- key derived skills: restDefenseReliability, tacticalDiscipline, ballSecurity.
- common skill profiles: late-match stabilization, transition stopping, support running.
- style compatibility: CONTROL uses safety; BLITZ uses transition relay.
- weakness if badly built: turnovers become clean opponent danger.
- best pairings: Tempo Half, Mobile Lock, Forward Leader.
- coach-facing explanation: the Pivot is what keeps ambition from becoming recklessness.
- CONTROL expression: central reset and rest-defense organizer.
- BLITZ expression: transition relay who supports vertical continuation with less positional stability.

## Left Piston

- plain-language description: left width, flank progression, and recovery role.
- tactical identity: two-way wide engine.
- in-possession behavior: supports width, switch reception, and flank progression.
- out-of-possession behavior: protects short side and recovers wide.
- transition behavior: supports wide attack or recovery depending ball side.
- chaos behavior: keeps width without reckless isolation.
- finishing behavior: supports wide entry, try support, and rebound follow-up.
- fatigue behavior: overlap and recovery frequency drop late.
- risk tolerance: medium.
- compatible scoring routes: weak-side switch, try support, shot setup.
- defensive responsibilities: flank cover and rest-defense repair.
- key visible attributes: Speed, Endurance, Hand Play, Ball Carrying, Vision.
- key derived skills: supportTiming, spacingQuality, recoveryRange.
- common skill profiles: support running, defensive recovery, endurance engine.
- style compatibility: CONTROL uses measured width; BLITZ uses vertical lane running.
- weakness if badly built: one flank becomes inactive or exposed.
- best pairings: Tempo Half, Playmaker, Right Piston.
- coach-facing explanation: the Left Piston keeps the left side useful in both directions.
- CONTROL expression: measured left width and short-side security.
- BLITZ expression: high-speed left-side runner who attacks depth and accepts more recovery load.

- finishing behavior: supports wide entry, try support, and rebound follow-up.
- fatigue behavior: overlap and recovery frequency drop late.
- risk tolerance: medium.
- compatible scoring routes: weak-side switch, try support, shot setup.
- defensive responsibilities: flank cover and rest-defense repair.
- key visible attributes: Speed, Endurance, Hand Play, Ball Carrying, Vision.
- key derived skills: supportTiming, spacingQuality, recoveryRange.
- common skill profiles: support running, defensive recovery, endurance engine.
- style compatibility: CONTROL uses measured width; BLITZ uses vertical lane running.
- weakness if badly built: one flank becomes inactive or exposed.
- best pairings: Tempo Half, Playmaker, Left Piston.
- coach-facing explanation: the Right Piston keeps the open side connected.
- CONTROL expression: measured right width and open-side connection.
