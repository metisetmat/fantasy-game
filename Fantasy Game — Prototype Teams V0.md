# PROTOTYPE_TEAMS.md

# Fantasy Game — Prototype Teams V0.1

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the official prototype teams used for:

* balancing;
* simulation testing;
* tactical validation;
* interaction validation;
* reporting validation.

These teams are intentionally extreme archetypes.

The goal is NOT realism.

The goal is to:

* stress-test the engine;
* reveal dominant systems;
* validate tactical readability;
* produce recognizable match identities.

---

# 2. Official V0.1 Prototype Teams

| Team          | Identity                     |
| ------------- | ---------------------------- |
| CONTROL       | possession & structure       |
| BLITZ         | transitions & pressing       |
| FORTRESS      | compact defense & resilience |
| CHAOS HUNTERS | aggression & disruption      |

---

# 3. Team Design Philosophy

Each prototype team must:

* possess clear strengths;
* possess exploitable weaknesses;
* create recognizable tactical patterns;
* generate different match stories.

No team should:

* dominate all matchups;
* excel in every phase;
* invalidate tactical diversity.

---

# 9. Validation Expectations

Prototype teams are successful if:

* their identities are visible in reports;
* their strengths emerge naturally;
* their weaknesses are exploitable;
* matchups feel different;
* tactical patterns remain readable.

---

# 10. Failure Conditions

Prototype teams fail if:

* all teams produce similar matches;
* styles are not recognizable;
* one philosophy dominates all others;
* reports fail to communicate identity;
* interactions ignore tactical instructions.

---

# 11. Final Philosophy

These prototype teams are NOT final game archetypes.

They are:

* balancing tools;
* tactical stress tests;
* emergence validators;
* simulation benchmarks.

Their purpose is to help the engine reveal:

* strengths;
* weaknesses;
* dominant systems;
* tactical clarity;
* emotional dynamics.
